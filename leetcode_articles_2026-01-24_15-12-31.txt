###### While practicing graph problems, I realized that many times I was choosing DFS or BFS randomly, without thinking about why that approach.
###### What helped me was focusing on what the problem was actually asking.
###### I found that if I only needed to check whether a path exists, a DFS Approach was usually enough. But was the problem asked for the shortest path or minimum steps, BFS was the better choice because it explores level by level traversals.

###### This conclusion helped me stop forcefully using DFS in problems where BFS was more appropriate and made my solutions less complicated.
